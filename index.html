Welcome to our Web

Geophysics is the branch of Earth science that uses physical principles and methods to study the Earth’s structure, composition, and processes. It involves applying physics—like gravity, magnetism, electricity, seismic waves, and heat flow—to understand what lies beneath the Earth's surface.
Key Areas of Geophysics:
1. Seismology – Studies earthquakes and seismic waves to explore Earth's interior.
2. Magnetometry – Measures magnetic fields to detect subsurface features.
3. Gravimetry – Uses gravity variations to identify structures like oil reservoirs or mineral deposits.
4. Electrical and Electromagnetic Methods – Explore subsurface resistivity to find groundwater, minerals, or archaeological features.
5. Remote Sensing & Satellite Geophysics – Observes the Earth from above to monitor changes and patterns.

Applications:
- Locating natural resources (oil, gas, minerals, water)
- Environmental and engineering site investigations
- Earthquake hazard analysis
- Studying Earth's internal structure
- Archaeological surveys
